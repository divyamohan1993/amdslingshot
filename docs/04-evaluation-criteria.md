# AMD Ryzen AI Slingshot - Evaluation Criteria

## Published Evaluation Dimensions

Solutions are evaluated by industry leaders and AMD professionals on three core pillars:

1. **Innovation** — Originality of the idea, creative use of AI/AMD technology
2. **Feasibility** — Technical soundness, working prototype, realistic implementation
3. **Impact** — Real-world relevance, scale of the problem addressed, social/business value

## Evaluation Process

### Stage 1: Idea Submission (Online)
- Teams submit idea proposals/prototypes through the Hack2Skill platform
- Evaluated for shortlisting — criteria not publicly detailed
- Shortlist announced on 16 March 2026

### Stage 2: Regional Demo Day (Offline, 10 Cities)
- "Exclusive for Slingshot Teams — Only shortlisted teams from the AMD Slingshot contest are eligible"
- Teams present prototypes to an expert jury of industry leaders and AMD professionals
- "Solutions are evaluated by industry leaders and AMD professionals"
- One team per city advances to the National Grand Finale

### Stage 3: National Grand Finale (Offline)
- Top-performing teams from all 10 cities compete nationally
- Top three winners selected
- Final judging by AMD professionals and industry experts

## Regional Ideathon Evaluation (Separate Track)
- Standalone one-day innovation sprint
- Same-day winner announcements
- Separate evaluation from the main Slingshot contest
- Dedicated prize pool exclusive to this track

## Key Notes

- **No formal scoring rubric with point allocations has been published** on the website
- Detailed evaluation criteria may be shared with shortlisted teams during the RSVP and Briefing phase (16–26 March 2026)
- The three stated pillars — Innovation, Feasibility, and Impact — are the primary publicly known judging parameters
- Judging panels consist of "industry leaders and AMD professionals"
